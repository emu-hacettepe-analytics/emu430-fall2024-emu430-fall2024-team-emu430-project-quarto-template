Analysis Overview:

This analysis seeks to explore and compare Turkey’s demographic indicators with those of more developed and less developed regions, based on data provided by the UN Demographic Indicators Dataset. By examining critical parameters such as total deaths, crude death rates, and fertility trends, we aim to gain valuable insights into Turkey’s demographic status in relation to other regions with varying levels of development.

We will examine these indicators over time to assess whether Turkey’s demographic patterns are more aligned with developed countries, which generally experience better healthcare outcomes, or with less developed countries, where healthcare access may be limited, resulting in different mortality and fertility trends.

Births by Women Aged 15-19: Turkey vs. More Developed vs. Less Developed Countries

This graph compares the birth rate among women aged 15-19 in Turkey, More Developed countries, and Less Developed countries.

This code block visualizes the graph:

library(readxl)
other_parameters <- read_excel("C:/Users/HP/Desktop/other_parameters.xlsx")
library(tidyverse)  # veya library(dplyr)
ldeveloped_data_other <- other_parameters %>%
  filter(Country == "Less developed regions")
turkey_data_other <- other_parameters %>%
  filter(Country == "Turkiye")
ldeveloped_data_other <- other_parameters %>%
  filter(Country == "Less developed regions")
mdeveloped_data_other <- other_parameters %>%
  filter(Country == "More developed regions")
library(ggplot2)
# Turkey veri setine Source sütunu ekleyin
data1 <- turkey_data_other
data1$Source <- "Turkey"

# More developed veri setine Source sütunu ekleyin
data2 <- mdeveloped_data_other
data2$Source <- "More developed"

data3 <- ldeveloped_data_other
data3$Source <- "Less developed"

# İki veri setini birleştirme
combined_data <- rbind(data1, data2,data3)
ggplot(combined_data, aes(x = Year, y = `Births by women aged 15 to 19 (thousands)`, color = Source)) +
  geom_line(linewidth = 1) +  # Çizgileri ekleyin
  labs(
    title = "Births by women aged 15 to 19 (thousands) Over Years",
    x = "Year",
    y = "Births by women aged 15 to 19 (thousands)"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

Suggested Interpretation:

  • Teen Birth Rates and Socioeconomic Factors: Teen births are often influenced by socioeconomic, educational, and healthcare factors. Less developed countries typically have higher rates of teen births due to lower access to education and family planning services. By comparing Turkey to both developed and less developed countries, we can analyze where Turkey stands in terms of policies surrounding family planning, education, and access to healthcare.

  • Trends in Turkey: If Turkey’s teen birth rates are closer to those of less developed countries, it might suggest that certain cultural or social factors, or lack of effective health education, contribute to higher teen pregnancies. On the other hand, if Turkey’s rates are closer to those of developed countries, it may reflect effective policy interventions such as better education for young women and improved access to contraception and reproductive health services.

  • Implications for Policy: The results could lead to insights on how Turkey can improve its healthcare and education systems to lower teen pregnancies. Comparative analysis with developed countries might reveal strategies and policies that Turkey could adopt to further reduce teen birth rates.

By examining the graph:

  • Turkey’s life expectancy appears to follow a similar upward trajectory as More Developed countries, especially in the recent years. This indicates that Turkey has made substantial progress in terms of healthcare and living conditions, aligning more closely with the developed regions.

  • Less Developed countries, on the other hand, generally show a slower increase in life expectancy. This suggests that they still face significant challenges in improving healthcare and living standards.

This analysis indicates that Turkey is steadily closing the gap with more developed regions, showcasing positive demographic developments in terms of life expectancy.
