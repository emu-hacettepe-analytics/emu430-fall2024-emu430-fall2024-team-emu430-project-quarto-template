Analysis Overview:

This analysis seeks to explore and compare Turkey’s demographic indicators with those of more developed and less developed regions, based on data provided by the UN Demographic Indicators Dataset. By examining critical parameters such as total deaths, crude death rates, and fertility trends, we aim to gain valuable insights into Turkey’s demographic status in relation to other regions with varying levels of development.

We will examine these indicators over time to assess whether Turkey’s demographic patterns are more aligned with developed countries, which generally experience better healthcare outcomes, or with less developed countries, where healthcare access may be limited, resulting in different mortality and fertility trends.

//////bu grafiklerin analizlerini yapmadım.

  • Trend Analysis: We can observe the trends in total deaths for each group across the years. By comparing the curves, we can analyze whether Turkey’s death rate aligns more closely with the trend of more developed countries or less developed ones. Factors like healthcare improvements, aging population, and socio-economic conditions may influence these trends.

  • Demographic Alignment: From a demographic perspective, we can assess if Turkey’s total deaths trend shows more similarity to that of more developed countries, which generally experience lower death rates due to better healthcare, or if it is closer to less developed countries with potentially higher mortality rates due to limited healthcare access.

  • Conclusions: Drawing on these trends, Turkey may either resemble developed countries more closely (suggesting improvements in health systems) or share characteristics with less developed regions (highlighting areas that need improvement in public health and healthcare access).
